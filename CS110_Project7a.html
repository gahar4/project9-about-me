Research Q: Between Gender, Nightmare Frequency, and Recent Life Changes- which factor has the strongest correlation with Psychological Problems at School?

Yes, I think my question can be answered with the available data and visualizations. I was surprised to not find much connection between nightmare frequency and psychological problems at school. Generally, the data was more evenly distributed than I had predicted.My analysis is limited because I didn't consider other factors, such as sleep patterns.

As shown by the box plot, the overall range and medians of psych problems to weekly, bi-weekly, and monthly frequencies are essentially the same. The histogram reflects this, and further shows that most participants in the study had weekly nightmares. It looks like there isn't much difference between gender and psych problems at school. It is clear, however, that having a recent life change has the strongest correlation with psych problems at school. Among children having nightmares, those who had a recent life change are drastically more likely to have any degree of psychological problems at school. Further investigation should look at the more specific data describing the life changes experienced.
